Asaf Pras

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Environmental Engineering at Tel Aviv University, At the Water-Energy Laboratory, holding a double major in Mechanical Engineering and Earth Sciences, with an emphasis on Environmental Studies. During my last year of undergraduate studies, I joined the direct program for an M.Sc. in the Environmental Engineering Program at the Mechanical Engineering faculty, which I then continued in a direct program toward a Ph.D.

My research, under the supervision of Prof. Hadas Mamane, focuses mainly on water quality modeling and analysis, where I apply data science techniques to develop tools and models to simplify complicated, time-consuming, and expansive water tests. This includes microbial and chemical water tests for drinking and recreational waters. In addition, as part of the research, we develop and construct real-time, in-situ, and affordable water-contact sensors for field tests. These sensors support and are being used for various research in low-middle resource communities.

I have a strong background in data science, machine learning, and deep neural networks, mainly on tabular data and multi-spectral imagery. My goal is to find, develop, and test innovative ways to understand better and quickly what is in the water, except for H2O.
